Gripper and Structure for a Service Robot

  • Design and 3D print a 2-finger parallel gripper able to hold up a variety of household objects.
  • Used a custom mold to create the silicone rubber pads for the gripper fingers, succesfully increasing friction and grip.
  • Manufacture of a metal cover to protect the internal components of the robot, designed using SolidWorks sheet metal features.
  • Used weldments features to design a new internal structure for the robot, maximizing its height without compromising stability, two more front wheels were added to achieve this while ensuring that the robot's LiDAR sensor was not obstructed.
  • Redesigned the robot's cover for the new structure, now manufactured with acrylic sheets which improved the paint finish.
  • Created and tested the robot's URDF and xacro files to be used in ROS2, created a node that publishes the gripper's IR sensor state.
  • Competed internationally in the RoboCup 2025 in the @Home category, showcasing the capabilities of the service robot.

Eco-friendly Toy Cart

  • Design of an eco-friendly toy cart, as part of the "Design and Analysis of Experiments in Engineering Innovation" college course.
  • Designed on SolidWorks using sheet metal features, the car was powered with a rubber band mechanism.
  • Used Design of Experiments (DOE) and JMP software to optimize the cart's maximum traveled distance while changing different variables.
  • Manufactured using laser cutting techniques on cardboard, allowing for easy assembly and disassembly without the need for adhesives.
  • Used the design thinking methodology to always have in mind the final user of the toy.
  • The 8 produced carts were donated to the social service "ITERAndo con Javer".
